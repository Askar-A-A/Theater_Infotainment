{% placeholder "history_title" or %}Our Rich History{% endplaceholder %}

{% placeholder "history_content" or %}

Our theater has a storied past, beginning with its grand opening in 1869 as one of Europe's most architecturally significant opera houses. Designed by renowned architects, the neo-Renaissance building quickly became a symbol of cultural excellence and artistic achievement.

Through the Belle Époque and early 20th century, legendary conductors and composers including Gustav Mahler and Richard Strauss helped establish our reputation for musical excellence and innovation. Many important works received their world premieres on our stage, cementing our place in musical history.

Despite challenges, including reconstruction after World War II, our theater has continued to thrive. In 2019, we celebrated 150 years of artistic achievement, with a renewed commitment to preserving our rich heritage while embracing innovation for the future.
