Salome

About the Performance

Based on Oscar Wilde's play of the same name, Richard Strauss's opera Salome is a powerful one-act drama that shocked audiences when it premiered in 1905. The opera tells the biblical story of Salome, stepdaughter of Herod, who develops an unhealthy obsession with the prophet Jochanaan (John the Baptist).

When Jochanaan rejects her advances, Salome performs the infamous "Dance of the Seven Veils" for her stepfather in exchange for Jochanaan's head on a silver platter. The opera culminates in Salome's disturbing final monologue as she addresses the severed head.

Strauss's revolutionary score features dissonant harmonies and modern tonality that perfectly captures the opera's themes of obsession, desire, and moral corruption. This production, directed by the acclaimed Emma Richardson, presents a contemporary vision of this classic work while honoring its musical complexity.

Director's Note

Our production of Salome explores the psychological dimensions of desire and rejection, power and helplessness. The minimalist staging allows the powerful music and performances to take center stage, while subtle lighting creates the oppressive atmosphere of Herod's palace.
