Frequently Asked Questions

Where can I rent an opera glass?
Opera glasses can be rented at the cloakroom for a small fee. A deposit may be required.
I lost something while attending a performance. Who can I turn to?
Please get in touch with our supervisory staff during the interval or after the performance at their offices in the foyer on the right-hand side of the grand staircase.

Should you fail to notice your loss until later, please contact Besucherservice der Wiener Staatsoper (in-house fire service) tel. +43-1-51444-2415 or kundenservice@wiener-staatsoper.at.
Can I also watch performances of Wiener Staatsoper live at home?
Yes, selected performances are available for streaming through our digital platform. Visit our streaming service website for more information.
Which benefits do I enjoy as a partner of Wiener Staatsoper?
Partners enjoy various benefits including priority booking, exclusive events, backstage tours, and special discounts. Contact our partnership office for detailed information.
What is the dress code for performances?
While there is no strict dress code, evening wear is customary and appreciated. Many guests choose formal attire: suits or tuxedos for gentlemen and evening dresses or elegant pantsuits for ladies. We recommend avoiding overly casual clothing out of respect for the performers and other guests.
Are there subtitles available during performances?
Yes, all performances feature digital subtitles in multiple languages. These are displayed on small screens on the back of each seat or above the stage. Languages typically include German and English, with additional languages available for selected performances.
How early should I arrive before the performance?
We recommend arriving at least 30 minutes before the performance begins. This allows time for parking, coat check, finding your seat, and settling in. The doors typically open 60 minutes before curtain time. Please note that latecomers may not be admitted until a suitable break in the performance.
Is photography allowed during performances?
Photography, video recording, and audio recording are strictly prohibited during all performances. This includes the use of mobile phones and other electronic devices. Photos may be taken in the foyer and other public areas before the performance and during intermissions.
Are there facilities for disabled visitors?
Yes, our theater is fully accessible to disabled visitors. We offer wheelchair spaces in the auditorium, accessible restrooms, and elevator access to all levels. Please inform our box office of any special requirements when booking your tickets so we can ensure appropriate arrangements are made.
Is there parking available at the theater?
While the theater does not have its own parking facility, there are several public parking garages within walking distance. We recommend the Opera Garage or City Parking. Special evening rates may apply during performance times. Valet parking service is also available for an additional fee.
Are refreshments available?
Yes, our theater features several bars and refreshment areas where you can purchase drinks and light snacks before the performance and during intermissions. We recommend pre-ordering your intermission refreshments to avoid queuing. Please note that food and drinks are not permitted in the auditorium.
What happens if I can't attend a performance I've booked?
Tickets cannot be refunded but may be exchanged for another performance (subject to availability) or returned for resale. Exchange fees may apply. Please contact our box office as soon as possible if you cannot attend. Season ticket holders have additional flexibility regarding exchanges.
